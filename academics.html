Competency Based Curriculum (CBC)

Aligned with Kenya's national education standards

At King Solomon Junior Primary School, we follow the Competency Based Curriculum (CBC) as prescribed by the Kenya Institute of Curriculum Development (KICD). Our curriculum is designed to develop learners' competencies, skills, and values that will enable them to thrive in the 21st century.

Assessment and Evaluation

Continuous assessment aligned with CBC requirements

Continuous Assessment Tests (CATs)

Regular assessments throughout the term to monitor progress and provide timely feedback to students and parents.

  • Weekly quizzes and tests
  • Project-based assessments
  • Practical demonstrations
  • Portfolio assessments

End of Term Examinations

Comprehensive examinations at the end of each term to evaluate overall understanding and mastery of competencies.

  • Written examinations
  • Practical assessments
  • Oral presentations
  • Creative projects

KNEC Assessments

Participation in national assessments conducted by the Kenya National Examinations Council (KNEC).

  • Grade 3 National Assessment
  • Grade 6 National Assessment
  • Standardized testing
  • National benchmarking

Assessment Philosophy

Our assessment approach focuses on competency development rather than rote memorization. We use a variety of assessment methods to capture different aspects of learning, including knowledge, skills, attitudes, and values. Assessment results are used to guide instruction, provide feedback, and support student learning.
